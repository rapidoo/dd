# D\&D Basic Adventure (1st level): The Forge of Rogbrok

<div class="post-header-line-1">

<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">

</div>

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-size: 12pt;">This is a small adventure for 1st-level characters, created entirely using the D\&D Basic Rules. It should give a group of four 1st level characters enough experience points to advance to second level. By adding or removing a few monsters, it could probably be a good challenge for more or fewer characters.</span><span style="font-size: 12pt;"><span lang="EN-US" style="font-size: 12pt;"></span></span></span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">
</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-size: 12pt;">Should you need, you can use the pre-generated characters found </span>[<span lang="EN-US" style="color: blue; font-size: 12pt;">here</span>](http://realmsofauria.blogspot.no/2014/10/gathering-party.html)<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-size: 12pt;">. </span></span>
<span id="more"></span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">**<span lang="EN-US" style="color: #990000; font-size: 18pt;">Background</span><span lang="EN-US" style="font-size: 18pt;"></span>**</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

|                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------: |

|                                                                                                                                                                                                                      *<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">Skirvir - Dwarven Miner</span>*                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |

<div style="text-align: left;">

</div>

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-size: 12pt;">At a local tavern, the adventurers run into an old, drunken [dwarven miner](http://realmsofauria.blogspot.no/2016/08/d-basic-npc-gallery-dwarf-commoner.html), Skirvir, boasting about having located an old dwarven mine, he is sure belonged to the long lost Jarmhammar  clan. In fact, he is sure he has found the location of the lost halls of Gwandatholir itself. </span></span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">
</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

[<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"></span>](https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEi6NjlileDZhyYSP4fA6s95xciXsuVhw06zPW17wujoB_AG8RU4wdVn-uEMu66DybjRBxMIRxWW3zQkzyCTeb9LirWIJd0gRdhLTbtyCTR7cIOnmnWZE-3HijYt6drn5ibEY6EoKM6VNf8/s1600/gwandatholir_text_box.jpg)<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-size: 12pt;">The old dwarf has not found the halls of Gwandatholir, but the mines he has found did once belong to a dwarf of the Jarn<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">hammar</span> clan; Rogbrok Jarn<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">hammar</span> - a bit of a renegade and outcast of the Jarn<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">hammar</span>s. Rogbrok was thrown out of the clan, and wandered the lands for years, until he settled for a while in the Rust hills, where he built an iron mine and a forge, before he moved on to do great heroic deeds, now sung about in ballads.</span></span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">
</span> <span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-size: 12pt;">A few drinks, and a successful Charisma (persuasion) **** DC 12, will loosen Skirvir's tongue, and he will let slip the location of the mines. He can also be persuaded to show the players the location of the mine entrance, and even accompany them in (though he is mostly useless in a fight), but in such case he will demand his own cut of any treasures found.</span><span lang="EN-US" style="font-size: 12pt;"></span></span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">
</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">**<span lang="EN-US" style="color: #990000; font-size: 18pt;">Rust Hill</span><span lang="EN-US" style="font-size: 18pt;"></span>**</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

[<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"></span>](https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEg_yWF2n1YpJllcnarewp0RhL0FikS_XcYk4KRh3nUKBpNWmnpZ5Qyc3pa2A2gf4twC5wVMdhH6aaPi1z2qA12F_pWbMonjlFngPvyRs6mMJvn2sWinebbM1uFKJMsQw-wNdN_KsdL88iQ/s1600/wanderlust.jpg)<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-size: 12pt;">If the players choose to go to the mines, give them opportunity to stock up in town for anything they might need. Skirvir (if asked) will suggest various mining supplies, such as a good light source, pick axes, shovels, plenty of rope, a canary and a ten foot pole. </span><span lang="EN-US" style="font-size: 12pt;"></span></span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">
</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-size: 12pt;">Rust Hill lies about one half day's march away from the town where the players met Skirvir. Getting there should be fairly uneventful, though to spice things up, the DM could roll for a random encounter on the way if desired.</span><span lang="EN-US" style="font-size: 12pt;"></span></span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">
</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-size: 12pt;">Finding the entrance to the cave shouldn't be too difficult, depending on how much information they managed to get out of Skirvir the evening before. If he is with them, Skirvir will lead the players more or less directly to the mine entrance, otherwise have the players roll a Wisdom (Perception or Survival) or Intelligence (Nature) DC 13. On a failure, they end up spending a few more hours searching for the entrance before they find it (with the possibility of another random encounter).</span><span lang="EN-US" style="font-size: 12pt;"></span></span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">
</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">A small stone shed stood where the entrance to the mines used to be. The shed is now nothing but ruins, and just a faint trace of the foundations remain. The entrance itself is a gaping hole in the hillside leading down into the earth. </span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">
</span>

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">**<span lang="EN-US" style="color: #990000; font-size: 18pt;">Mines Description</span><span lang="EN-US" style="font-size: 18pt;"></span>**</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-size: 12pt;">The mines have been abandoned for a long time, its resources having been spent. Recently however a small band of Kobolds have entered the mines, with the hope of finding some ore. </span><span lang="EN-US" style="font-size: 12pt;"></span></span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">
</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-size: 12pt;">Except when noted, there are no light sources inside. The ceiling is supported by old, but sturdy beams, and was solidly built, and there should generally not be any danger of collapse. Except for the forge (area 3 and 4) the walls and floor is roughly hewn, with pebbles and rocks strewn about. This does not create difficult terrain, except when noted.</span><span lang="EN-US" style="font-size: 12pt;"></span></span>

</div>

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">**<span class="underline"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: #990000; font-size: 13.5pt;">1. Bear Cave</span></span><span lang="EN-US" style="font-size: 13.5pt;"></span>**</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">The tunnel from the surface descends steeply into the first chamber of the mine, where a large [**Brown Bear**](http://realmsofauria.blogspot.no/2016/04/d-basic-monsters-brown-bear.html) has made its lair. Anyone searching can make a DC12 Wisdom (Perception) to notice large tracks around the entrance, and a DC13 Intelligence (Nature) will identify the tracks as bear tracks.</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">
</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">The descent might look deceptively easy, but anyone going down without taking any precautions must make a DC12 Dexterity save, or slip and slide down to the bottom, taking 1d6-3 points of damage (where 0 or less means the character takes no damage). A character can choose to slide down voluntarily, taking only 1d4-3 damage. If a character takes any damage this way, he or she will fall prone at the bottom.</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">
</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">Anyone can make a DC 5 Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) to climb down without any problems, and using a rope gives advantage to this check.</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">

<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">The **Brown Bear** will however not be very happy with intruders to its lair, and will attack. Should the players find alternative ways of getting the bear out of its lair, without killing it (such as luring it away with food, or smoking it out with fire), award full XP (200) for the encounter. Just be aware that the bear might be bck in its lair by the time the players want to leave the mines.</span>

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">**<span class="underline"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: #990000; font-size: 13.5pt;">2. High vaulted cave</span></span><span lang="EN-US" style="font-size: 13.5pt;"></span>**</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

[<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"></span>](https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEj51uW7zkXmHKeASwEOoeV6vFSChU41tyq2_iq949srjTjKIdZ54gU1mPLvL0HzRoTjBuIZ-R2qj5A8_o35KQRD543ir_Ssrndly72es_M-iBuuR2BNvVlpa6codRuPmYGz7f68v2MQtL0/s1600/stirge_simple.jpg)<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-size: 12pt;">The ceiling is very high in this chamber, nearly 20 feet, and the room is dimly lit by small cracks in the roof (unless it's night time, in which case the cracks are invisible). These cracks are used by **four [Stirges](http://realmsofauria.blogspot.no/2014/11/d-basic-monsters-stirge.html)** to gain access to the outside. </span><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-size: 12pt;">Sensing blood, the **Stirges** will attack anyone entering the room.</span></span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-size: 12pt;"> </span><span lang="EN-US" style="font-size: 12pt;"></span> </span>

</span></span> <span style="font-size: 12pt;"><span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">Off to the south rhythmic hammering and tapping can be heard from a tunnel leading of that direction, while another tunnel leads north.</span></span>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">
</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

[<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"></span>](https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEjLPL7NWdm-8D1t-9GDy_5fB_tzyoFf-yOAuLk4kyozkX09F2DxO161hxYSYD-bS23P_8I1GShiMGEpnDJhlmOLNzKVTUbNA1x5rIdS6omn9B0OE5DiuiLn4LOGH5HOICVmJ9IREWHTKCo/s1600/giant_lizard.jpg)<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">**<span class="underline"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: #990000; font-size: 13.5pt;">8. Mines</span></span><span lang="EN-US" style="font-size: 13.5pt;"></span>**</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">If the characters move down this corridor, the hammering noise becomes louder. As the corridor curves round, it opens up into a larger chamber, with several tunnels leading off in different directions. In the center of the room the players can see a [**Giant Lizard**](http://realmsofauria.blogspot.no/2015/02/d-basic-monsters-giant-lizard.html) with two large baskets across its back. The lizard is used by the kobolds to carry off ore from the mines to the main chamber.</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">
</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">Upon seeing the characters, the lizard will growl loudly, alerting the four [**Kobolds**](http://realmsofauria.blogspot.no/2014/11/d-basic-monsters-kobold.html) working in the tunnels, and then charge. The kobolds will arrive after one round, and attack from the various tunnels, preferring ranged attacks.</span>

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"><span class="underline"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: #990000; font-size: 12pt;">**Treasure**</span></span><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-size: 12pt;"> </span></span>
<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;"><span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">In the bags across the Giant Lizard's back are five nuggets of semi <span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">prec<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">ious metals, that can <span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">be sold on for 1 gp a pi<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">ece, in addi<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">tion to a large jasper ston<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">e, w<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">orth 25 gold<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"> pieces.</span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span></span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"></span>

</div>

</div>


<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">The tunnels beyond are all dead ends, but if you want, and the players insist on exploring them, you can use the following table as the characters progress inwards and downwards:</span>


<span class="underline">**<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;"><span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"><span class="underline">Tunnel en<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">counters</span></span></span> (roll 2d<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"><span class="underline">4</span></span>):</span>**</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNoSpacing">

<span lang="EN-US" style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">   8            <span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"><span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">Cave in (<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">2d6 points of damage from falling rocks, Dex save DC<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">14 for half)</span></span></span></span></span>

</div>


<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">**<span class="underline"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: #990000; font-size: 13.5pt;">9. Barred Corridor</span></span>**</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">This corridor continues only a short way until it stops, having been boarded up. The area proved unsafe, with the roof collapsing, so the dwar<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">ve</span>s chose to close off the corridor. After the barrier was raised, even more earth and rock has come loose,held back by the barricade.</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">
</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"><span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-size: 12pt;">Anyone carefully inspecting the barrier will, with a DC 10 Wisdom (Perception) check, realizes that it is bulging out at the center, possibly suggesting that some force is pushing on it from the other side. The characters can pry open the barricade using pry bars or similar tools, or hack it open with axes, picks or hammers. Weakening the barrier will however cause the earth and rocks that have piled up on the other side, to come tumbling out in an avalanche. Any character  within 10 feet of the blockade must make a DC13 Dexterity save or take 5 (2d4) points of bludgeoning damage, or half if successful. Characters right next to the barrier will also be partially buried in earth and rocks, effectively being *restrained* until they use an action to free themselves. Characters more than 5 feet away from the blockade have advantage on their saving throw, and take no damage if successful</span><span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">.</span></span></span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">
</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">Along with all the earth and rocks, the skeletal remains of two dwarven miners trapped in a rock fall, will come tumbling out, interspersed among the rocks. These will assemble as two [**<span style="font-family: &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">S</span>keletons**](http://realmsofauria.blogspot.no/2015/01/d-basic-monsters-skeleton.html) that will attack anything living on the other side.</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">
</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">**<span class="underline"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: #990000; font-size: 13.5pt;">10. Access to opening mechanism to forge room.</span></span><span lang="EN-US" style="font-size: 13.5pt;"></span>**</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">Beyond the barricade there <span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">do<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">esn't</span></span> seem<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"> to<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"> <span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">be</span></span></span> much of use for the characters. The further corridor beyond has collapsed completely, and without some serious mining, probably taking weeks or months, any further progress is impossible.</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">
</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">However, carefully searching the bit of corridor that was cleared <span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">when</span> the characters removed the barrier, a DC 15 Wisdom (Perception) will reveal a square hatch in the ceiling, just big enough for one person to climb into. After ascending about 20 feet, the tunnel opens up into a fairly narrow space, about two feet wide, but six feet long, extending another 10 feet up. This is the mechanism to the door leading into Rogbrok's forge (**<span class="underline">area <span style="font-family: &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">4</span></span>**) that jammed. The characters can make out weighted pulleys on wires, various stone wheels and such that makes the door open. Apparently loose rocks and rubble has jammed the mechanism, stopping the door from opening properly.</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">
</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">Allow the characters t<span style="font-family: &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">he</span> appropriate Intelligence or Wisdom checks, to realize that while they can probably remove the obstructions easily, th<span style="font-family: &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">is</span> will cause the massive stone door, directly above to descend straight onto them. There is no absolute solution to this, but reward players who come up with clever ways of removing the obstructions without getting caught under the door as it opens. (Solutions might include using spells such as *Mage Hand* while still in the tunnel under the door, or pulling obstructing rocks out using ropes from a safe distance).</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">
</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">Should a character be inside the chamber once the door comes down, he or she must succeed a DC 1<span style="font-family: &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">4</span><span style="font-family: &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"></span> Dexterity save or take 1<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">7</span> (<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">5</span>d6) points of damage, or half on a successful save.</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">
</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">Once the mechanism has been cleared, and the door has opened, the characters can make their way back up again to enter the forge (**<span class="underline">area <span style="font-family: &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">5</span></span>**). </span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">
</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">**<span class="underline"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: #990000; font-size: 13.5pt;">Concluding the adventure.</span></span><span lang="EN-US" style="font-size: 13.5pt;"></span>**</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

|                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------: |
|  |
|                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Rogbrok Jarnhammer's sigil.                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-size: 12pt;">While the characters may not leave the mines with quite as many riches, or as many legendary artifacts as they may have wished for, the items left in Rogbrok's forge will still be worth a bit.  </span></span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">


<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">Furthermore, the DM is free to plant hints to further adventures within, such as clues to other, richer mines, monster lairs or clues to where the legendary dwarven hero Rogbrok went after he abandoned his workshop.</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span style="font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif;">
</span>

</div>

<div class="MsoNormal" style="line-height: normal;">

<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">In addition, once the mines are cleared, they characters could adopt the forge as a base or safe house. If they do so, they might get a surprise from its former owner. Rogbrok may have been gone for a hundred years, </span><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">or more, but being a dwarf that only puts him in his middle years, so the legendary hero could still be around...</span></span>
<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">
</span></span> <span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">That concludes the adventure.</span></span>
<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">
</span></span> <span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">If you need, you can find all the maps for the adventure on one printable sheet [here](http://realmsofauria.blogspot.com/2017/10/forge-of-rogrbrok-maps.html), and printable and unmarked maps are available [here](https://realmsofauria.blogspot.com/2020/12/forge-of-rogbrok-maps-bonanza.html).</span></span>
<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">

<span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;"><span lang="EN-US" style="color: black; font-family: georgia, &quot;times new roman&quot;, serif; font-size: 12pt;">
